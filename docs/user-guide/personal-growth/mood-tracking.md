# Mood Tracking

Understanding your emotional patterns is key to better communication. Meedi8's mood tracking helps you build emotional awareness over time.

---

## Why Track Your Mood?

### For Mediation
- **Recognize patterns** - Notice what triggers certain moods
- **Better timing** - Choose when you're emotionally ready to mediate
- **Self-awareness** - Understand how your mood affects communication
- **Track progress** - See how mediation improves your wellbeing

### For Personal Growth
- **Emotional intelligence** - Become more aware of your feelings
- **Identify trends** - Spot patterns over days and weeks
- **Early intervention** - Catch low moods before they spiral
- **Celebrate wins** - Acknowledge good days too

---

## How to Access

1. Open the **Navigation Panel** (tap the menu button)
2. Select **Personal Growth**
3. Tap **Log Mood**

You can also log mood from the **Wellness** tab on your dashboard.

## Logging a Mood Check-in

### Step 1: Select Your Mood
Tap the weather icon that matches how you're feeling right now.

### Step 2: Rate Your Energy Level
Slide the energy scale from 1 (exhausted) to 5 (energised).

### Step 3: Add a Note (Optional)
Write a brief note about what's contributing to your mood. This helps you identify patterns later.

**Example notes:**
- "Had a good conversation with partner this morning"
- "Stressed about work deadline"
- "Feeling better after yesterday's mediation"

### Step 4: Submit
Tap **Save** to record your check-in.

## Viewing Mood History

Access your mood history from the **Wellness** tab on your dashboard:

- **Calendar view** - See moods day by day
- **Trends** - Notice patterns over time
- **Notes** - Review what contributed to each mood

---

## Context-Aware Check-ins

Meedi8 may prompt you to log your mood at meaningful moments:

- **Before mediation** - How are you feeling going in?
- **After mediation** - Did the session help?
- **Daily reminders** - Build a consistent habit

These contextual check-ins help you see how mediation affects your emotional wellbeing.

## Tips for Effective Mood Tracking

:::tip Be Honest
There are no wrong answers. The more honest you are, the more valuable your mood data becomes for spotting patterns.
:::

:::tip Track Consistently
Try to log at the same time each day - morning or evening works well. Consistency reveals patterns.
:::

:::tip Use Notes
Brief notes make your mood history much more useful. Even "tired" or "good chat" adds context.
:::

:::tip Review Weekly
Take a few minutes each week to review your mood history. Look for patterns and triggers.
:::
